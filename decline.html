The Vanished Empire

By 1900 BCE, the great cities began to crumble. How does a superpower simply disappear?

The Drying River (Drought)

Evidence suggests the monsoon patterns shifted and the mighty Saraswati river dried up. Without water for their massive grain surpluses, the urban centers could no longer support thousands of citizens.

Migration & Conflict

The 'Aryan Invasion' theory suggests nomadic tribes from Central Asia entered through the Khyber Pass. While debated, many believe a mix of cultural shift and conflict led to the abandonment of Harappan traditions.

Ecological Collapse

Massive, repetitive flooding of the Indus River may have weakened the foundations of Mohenjo-Daro. The "Overall Decline" saw a breakdown in civic order, standardized weights, and the end of the script.

The Final Mystery

There was no proves of final battle. No great fire. The people simply walked away, leaving their toys, their jewelry, and their history behind. They returned to small village life, and the secret of their writing was lost for 4,000 years.

"The silence of the Indus remains unbroken."
